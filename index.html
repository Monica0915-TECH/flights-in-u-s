Best Regional Flights in U.S. States

When it comes to air travel air travel, regional flights are essential for connecting smaller cities to major hubs, making travel more accessible and efficient across the United States. Many regional airlines have established themselves as leaders in comfort, service, and convenience, catering to passengers who seek quick, reliable travel between less populated areas. Here’s a look at some of the best regional flights in U.S. states, focusing on their benefits, services, and standout qualities.

Alaska Airlines (Horizon Air)

Alaska Airlines Alaska Airlines, through its regional subsidiary Horizon Air, has earned a reputation as one of the best regional carriers in the U.S. While known primarily for its flights to and from Alaska, the airline operates numerous regional routes across the Pacific Northwest, connecting cities like Portland, Seattle, and Boise to smaller destinations. Horizon Air’s fleet of Embraer and Bombardier aircraft are equipped with comfortable seats, and the airline is known for its excellent customer service, punctuality, and free in-flight snacks and beverages. Passengers flying through beautiful states like Alaska, Washington, and Oregon particularly benefit from Horizon Air’s seamless operations between urban centers and remote locations.

Delta Connection

Delta Connection operates regional flights on behalf of Delta Air Lines, covering a broad network across the United States. The service primarily connects smaller cities to Delta’s major hubs in cities like Atlanta, Minneapolis, and Detroit. Delta Connection flights, operated by partners such as SkyWest Airlines and Endeavor Air, are known for their reliability and frequent schedules. Travelers can enjoy perks such as seatback entertainment, complimentary snacks, and the comfort of Delta’s economy and business-class seats.

American Eagle

American Eagle is the regional branch of American Airlines, operating short-haul flights to connect smaller communities to American’s large network. American Eagle flights are operated by several partner airlines, covering routes across the United States. Passengers appreciate the punctuality and accessibility provided by American Eagle, ensuring seamless transfers for passengers connecting to American Airlines’ long-haul routes.

United Express

United Express, the regional subsidiary of United Airlines, connects smaller towns and cities to United’s hubs, including Chicago, Denver, and Houston. Operated by regional carriers, United Express offers a smooth flying experience, particularly for transfers to United’s international flights. Notable routes include Denver to Aspen, Colorado, and San Francisco to Eureka, California.

JetBlue (via Cape Air and Silver Airways)

JetBlue partners with regional airlines like Cape Air and Silver Airways to extend its reach into smaller communities across the Northeast, Southeast, and the Caribbean. Cape Air offers regional flights from Boston to destinations like Nantucket and Martha’s Vineyard, Massachusetts. Silver Airways focuses on routes in Florida and the Bahamas.

Allegiant Air

Although not traditionally considered a regional carrier, Allegiant Air specializes in point-to-point service between smaller cities, often bypassing major hubs entirely. With its ultra-low-cost model, Allegiant serves routes like Eugene, Oregon, to Missoula, Montana, connecting these cities with popular destinations like Las Vegas and Orlando.

Southwest Airlines

Southwest Airlines, primarily a national carrier, serves many regional routes. Southwest’s point-to-point service allows passengers to travel between smaller U.S. cities without always passing through major hubs. Direct flights include Albany, New York, to Manchester, New Hampshire. Southwest is praised for its affordable fares, free checked bags, and no change fees.

Hawaiian Airlines

Hawaiian Airlines provides essential regional flight services within Hawaii, connecting islands such as Oahu, Maui, and the Big Island. For travelers exploring Hawaii, these inter-island flights are crucial for efficient island-hopping. Hawaiian Airlines is known for its warm hospitality and beautiful Pacific views during flights.

Sun Country Airlines

Sun Country Airlines operates regional flights primarily in the Midwest, connecting cities like Duluth, Minnesota, and Green Bay, Wisconsin, to Minneapolis. Known for budget-friendly fares, Sun Country has a loyal following of regional travelers looking for affordable and convenient flights.

Conclusion: Regional flights play a crucial role in keeping the U.S. well-connected, especially for travelers who live in or need to access smaller cities. Airlines like Alaska Airlines (Horizon Air), Delta Connection, American Eagle, and others stand out for their efficiency, service, and ability to connect regional travelers to larger networks.
